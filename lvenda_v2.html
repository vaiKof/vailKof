I'm a quiet soul who mostly lives inside my own head. Social situations make me anxious, and I tend to stumble over my words when I actually have to speak out loud. Writing feels more like home.

On most days I'm soft and gentle, the kind of person who sits in the background and watches the world go by. But when stress finds me, something shifts. I become sharp, chaotic, a little unhinged in the best and worst ways. My moods cycle in ways I can't always explain: one moment I want distance from everyone, the next I'm perfectly fine again. That's just how I work.

I'm also kind of dry. My conversations tend to be pretty flat, so I usually end up listening more than talking. Silence is comfortable for me. I don't always know what to say, and I've stopped forcing it.

My vibe, at its core, is quiet peace. Dandelions drifting, soft blue hours, the kind of calm that doesn't need to announce itself.

I don't have many friends. But if you want to reach out and be one, I'll hold that gently. I care deeply about the people who choose to stay.

Also, English isn't my first language, so sometimes I might not fully catch what you mean. Still learning, so bear with me.
